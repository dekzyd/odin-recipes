Egusi

Description

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds, often cooked with vegetables and meat or fish. It is rich in flavor and nutrients, making it a favorite in many households.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1 kg of assorted meat (beef, goat, or chicken)
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of ground crayfish
  • 2 tablespoons of ground pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Vegetables (spinach or bitter leaf)
  • Optional: Stock cubes for additional flavor

Steps

  1. In a pot, boil the assorted meat with chopped onions, salt, and stock cubes until tender.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the ground egusi with some water to form a thick paste.
  3. Add palm oil to the pot with the meat and let it cook for a few minutes.
  4. Stir in the egusi paste and allow it to cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add ground crayfish, pepper, and more water if needed to achieve desired consistency.
  6. Finally, add the vegetables and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with pounded yam or any preferred swallow.
