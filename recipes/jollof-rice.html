Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It is often served at parties and celebrations, known for its rich flavor and vibrant color.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain rice
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cups of blended tomatoes and peppers
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • Optional: diced vegetables (carrots, peas)
  • Optional: protein (chicken, beef, or fish)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add the blended tomatoes and peppers to the pot, along with the tomato paste. Cook for about 10-15 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, ensuring that the rice is fully submerged. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  7. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  8. If using, add diced vegetables and protein during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.
